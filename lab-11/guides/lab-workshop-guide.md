### Demo Video Best Practices

**DO:**
- ‚úÖ Show your actual MVP (not mockups)
- ‚úÖ Follow a real user journey
- ‚úÖ Keep cursor movements smooth
- ‚úÖ Narrate what you're clicking
- ‚úÖ Show the problem being solved
- ‚úÖ Keep it under 90 seconds

**DON'T:**
- ‚ùå Explain features that aren't built yet
- ‚ùå Ramble or go off script
- ‚ùå Show bugs or incomplete features
- ‚ùå Use filler words ("um", "so", "like")
- ‚ùå Go over 90 seconds

**Template Script:**

```markdown

## [0-10s] Hook

## [10-40s] Core Flow
[Screen: Homepage]
"Open [product]."

"[Action 1 - show the key feature]."

[Screen: Result]
"[Show the outcome - problem solved]."

## [40-60s] Key Features
[Screen: Feature 2]
"You can also [secondary feature]."

[Screen: Feature 3]
"And [tertiary feature]."

## [60-90s] CTA
"[Product name]: [One-sentence benefit]. Try it at [URL]."
```

## üöâ STATION 6: Peer Review (1:45-2:00)

### 15-Minute Feedback Sprint

**Goal:** Get quick feedback, catch obvious issues

**Format:** Pair with another team

**Process:**

**Step 1: Swap Decks (2 min)**
- Team A shares screen with their deck
- Team B opens their deck

**Step 2: Team A Presents (5 min)**
- Quick walkthrough (don't read every word)
- Hit main points of each slide
- Show demo video

**Step 3: Team B Gives Feedback (3 min)**

Use this framework:

**‚úÖ What's Working:**
- "Slide [X] is really strong because..."
- "Your [traction/demo/problem] is compelling"
- "I love how you..."

**üîß What Needs Work:**
- "Slide [X] is confusing because..."
- "Can't read text on Slide [Y]"
- "Missing: [critical element]"

**üí° One Quick Win:**
- "If you could only fix one thing, fix..."

**Step 4: Swap (5 min)**
- Team B presents
- Team A gives feedback

### Quick Checklist During Peer Review

**Content:**
- [ ] Problem is clear and compelling
- [ ] Solution obviously solves the problem
- [ ] Demo shows actual working product
- [ ] Traction metrics are real (not aspirational)
- [ ] Team slide shows qualifications
- [ ] Ask is specific and realistic

**Design:**
- [ ] Text is readable from 10 feet away
- [ ] No walls of text (max 3-4 bullets per slide)
- [ ] Visuals support the story
- [ ] Consistent fonts and colors
- [ ] Professional (not overly designed)

**Flow:**
- [ ] Story builds logically
- [ ] Each slide connects to previous
- [ ] Timing feels right (~1 min per slide)

## üÜò Troubleshooting

### "We're running behind schedule"

**If you're at 60 min and only have 5 slides:**
- Skip to Station 4: Record demo video first (most important!)
- Then finish slides 6-10 quickly (use bullet points, polish later)
- Peer review can be homework if needed

**If you're at 90 min and don't have demo video:**
- Record a 30-second version (just show ONE key feature)
- Plan to expand it in Week 13
- OR: Plan to do live demo and add note to Slide 4

### "Our MVP isn't working"

**Option 1: Screenshot Demo**
- Take screenshots of your best screens
- Create a "fake" walkthrough using screenshots
- Narrate over the screenshots
- Less impressive but better than nothing

**Option 2: Explain the Vision**
- Slide 4 becomes: "What We're Building"
- Show mockups/wireframes from Lab 6
- Be honest: "In development, launching [date]"
- Demo Day judges will understand

### "We don't have good metrics"

**Use what you have:**
- Number of interviews (Lab 2-4)
- Landing page signups (Lab 5)
- Validation experiments (Labs 8-9)
- Even small numbers are okay if honest

**Frame it right:**
- "Validated through 15 interviews" ‚úÖ
- Not: "Only talked to 15 people" ‚ùå

### "Team disagreement on content"

**Quick vote:**
- 2 minutes discussion max
- Vote
- Majority wins
- Move on

**Remember:** Version 1 doesn't need to be perfect. You have 2 more weeks to refine!
